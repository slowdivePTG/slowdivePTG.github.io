\lettersection{Why Northwestern? - Academic Interests}
Northwestern University is one of the world’s centers for astronomy. The breadth of astrophysical research makes interdisciplinarity possible at CIERA. Powerful computational methods are applied to model construction for a variety of compact objects, stellar clusters, galaxies, and large-scale structure, to understand the data from the most advanced instrument in an era of multi-messenger astronomy. For graduate students at Northwestern, both research and course studying are highly emphasized. Students will be trained in physics, mathematics, and computer science, and will have access to diverse cutting-edge knowledge in astrophysics on seminars and conferences as well. 

I am not too surprised to find that Northwestern meets so perfectly with my research. Fascinated by modeling high-energy stellar events, I do appreciate \textbf{Prof. Frederic Rasio}’s theoretical work on the dynamics of compact objects in dense star clusters. \textbf{Prof. Alexander Tchekhovskoy}’s computational research on relativistic jets from accreting black holes and millisecond pulsars with MHD simulation is also what I am eager to explore. In addition, with experience in observations of transients, I am also interested in \textbf{Prof. Wen-fai Fong}’s work in multi-messenger astronomy searching for electromagnetic counterparts to gravitational wave sources, and \textbf{Prof. Raffaella Margutti}’s study on supernovae, GRB events, and their mechanisms, with deep observations from UV to gamma-rays. Besides, after exploring the molecular environment around Sgr A* for over a year, I do admire \textbf{Prof. Farhad Yusef-Zadeh}’s study on the impacts of an active, flaring supermassive black hole on our galaxy.

I do appreciate it if you could consider my application. I am looking forward to meeting with you for a more detailed talk.

I am Chang Liu (刘畅).
I was born and raised up in Xinchang, Shaoxing (绍兴, 新昌) in South-east China.
I am currently an undergrad in Department of Astronomy, Peking University.

Research

Aiming at developing an isotropic love and understanding to astronomy,
I am always ready to embrace different areas in astronomy and astrophysics.

During my Undergraduate Research & Training Program in Peking University, I have been working as an 'archaeologist' to study Milky Way's biochemical history. Using numerical methods, I have been trying to figure out the chemical impacts of a hypothetical AGN on the molecular clouds in the galactic disk.

I'm also interested in stellar binaries, black holes and gravitational radiation. In the summer of 2019, I will touch the field of trasnsients and work on light curves from Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) in Caltech to a conduct systematic search for periodic white dwarfs.

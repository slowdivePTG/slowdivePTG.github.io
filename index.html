I am Chang Liu (刘畅). I was born and raised up in Xinchang, Shaoxing (绍兴, 新昌) in South-east China. I am currently an undergraduate in Department of Astronomy, Peking University.

Aiming at developing an 'isotropic' love and understanding to astronomy, I am always ready to embrace various exciting areas in astronomy and astrophysics. During my Undergraduate Research & Training Program in Peking University, I work as an 'archaeologist' to study Milky Way's biochemical history. Using numerical methods, I have been trying to figure out the chemical impacts of a hypothetical AGN (which is believed to exist in the center of our Milky Way galaxy several million years ago) on the molecular clouds in the galactic disk.

Besides, I'm also interested in stellar binaries, black holes and gravitational radiation. In the summer of 2019, I will touch the field of trasnsient and work on light curves from Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) in Caltech to a conduct systematic search for periodic white dwarfs.

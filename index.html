Who Am I?

    I am Chang Liu (刘畅).
    My name is pronounced this way.
    I am a Astronomy PhD student in Northwestern University.
    I received my Bachelor of Science in Astronomy from Peking University.

Where Am I Going?

    One of my greatest pursuits is to combine state-of-the-art observations with powerful computational methods to understand the transient universe. This sweet 'temptation' has driven me to explore both observational and computational astrophysics from the violent termination of dead stars to objects torn apart by supermassive black holes. Some of my past researches are listed in Science.

Where Did I Come From?

    I was born in Xinchang (新昌), China, a mountainous, peaceful county where I used to wander as all those ancient Chinese poets did. I was then fascinated by the nature for the first time during star-counting and daydreaming, long before I picked up physics. I have inevitably become a shutterbug (my Instagram) and kept reading, wandering, and writing as my hobbies. Drop by my Gallery!

Chang Liu (刘畅 )

Time-Domain Astronomer

Who Am I?

I am a PhD candidate at Northwestern University , working with Prof. Adam Miller.

I received my Bachelor of Science in Astronomy from Peking University .

Where Am I Going?

One of my greatest pursuits is to combine state-of-the-art observations with powerful computational methods to understand the transient universe. I have been exploring both observational and computational astrophysics from the violent termination of dead stars to objects torn apart by supermassive black holes. Some of my research highlights are listed in Science.

Where Did I Come From?

I was born in Xinchang (新昌), China, a mountainous, peaceful county where I used to wander as all those ancient Chinese poets did. I was then fascinated by the nature for the first time during star-counting and daydreaming, long before I picked up any physics formula. I have inevitably become a shutterbug (my Adobe Portfolio) and could never stop reading, wandering, and writing. Drop by my Gallery!

Who Am I?

    I am Chang Liu (刘畅).
    My name is pronounced this way.
    I am a astronomy PhD student in Northwestern University. I received my Bachelor of Science (Hon) from Department of Astronomy, Peking University.

Where Am I Going?

    One of my greatest pursuits is to combine state-of-the-art observations with powerful computational methods to understand complex transient universe. This sweet 'temptation' has driven me to explore observational and computational astrophysics from astrochemistry within interstellar medium to binary systems in a large variety of scales, from ultra-compact double white dwarfs to stars orbiting around supermassive black holes. Some of my past researches are listed in Science.

Where Did I Come From?

    I was born in Xinchang (新昌), China, a mountainous, peaceful county where I used to wander as all those Chinese ancient poets did, counting stars and daydreaming, and was then fascinated by the nature for the first time, long before I picked up physics. I have inevitably become a shutterbug (my Instagram) and kept reading, wandering, and writing as my hobbies. Drop by my Gallery!

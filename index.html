I am Chang Liu (刘畅). I was born and raised up in Xinchang, Shaoxing (绍兴, 新昌) in South-east China. I am currently an undergraduate in Department of Astronomy, Peking University.



Aiming at developing an 'isotropic' love and understanding to astronomy, I am always ready to embrace various exciting areas in astronomy and astrophysics. During my Undergraduate Research & Training Program in Peking University, I work as an 'archaeologist' to study Milky Way's biochemical history. Using numerical methods, I have been trying to figure out the chemical impacts of a hypothetical AGN on the molecular clouds in the galactic disk.

I'm also interested in stellar binaries, black holes and gravitational radiation. In the summer of 2019, I will touch the field of trasnsients and work on light curves from Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF) in Caltech to a conduct systematic search for periodic white dwarfs. For more details, please click Research.

Apart from my research, I am also fascinated by anything which may allow me to speak to myself - all those pure and interesting issues, including photography, classic & rock music and literature. Some of my works are exhibited in the Gallery. My new (non-academic and capricious) writings are posted below, including but not limited to travel journals, music recommendations and surrealistic thoughts. Please feel free to drop by!
